Vitakka

This piece was commissioned by Heidelberger Frühling (the Heidelberg Festival) in 2017. I was a member of their Composition Academy that year, along with other student composers and performers.

The title, Vitakka, is a word from the ancient language of Pali; a language that would have been spoken by the Buddha. The word translates as 'application of thought'.

Vitakka is scored for cello and piano. The premiere was given by Domenico Milone on cello, with Roman Lopatynskyi on piano.

Whilst a member of the Composition Academy at Heidelberger Frühling, I also composed a piece for the Divan of Song concert series, set up by German pianist, Burkhard Kehring. To read more about the song that I composed for the Divan of Song, see Auspicious Bliss.
