Night Bazaar

Night Bazaar was an exploration of new compositional techniques that I was learning whilst a student. The piece began, characteristically of me, without any thought of structure. I was simply experimenting with these new techniques, trying to discover if there were any good seeds to water.

As structure wasn't much of a primary thought, I quite simply divided the piece up into sections of fast, slow, fast, slow, etc., and the piece culminates with a long, winding melody, passed between different instruments in the ensemble.

The title was only arrived after completing the composition. I wasn't thinking about busy bazaars, day or night, whilst I was composing this. But I still think the title suits the music.

This piece was composed for mixed ensemble and performed by students at a concert at the Royal Academy of Music.
