Unvoiced

The initial inspiration for this piece came after reflecting on the last few years of my grandfather's life, during which he was suffering from the severe effects of dementia. At that point in his life, he no longer recognised his own family, and coherent conversation with him was hard to come by. But occasionally his verbal wanderings were penetrated by moments of clarity, where his speech was clear and his memory was sharp.

In this piece there are A and B sections. The A sections reflect wandering speech. It's free and flowing, and develops organically throughout the piece. The B sections are measured and altogether more regular in terms of rhythm. These sections also occur in a 3/8 metre, reminiscent of the dance movements of the 16th and 17th Centuries.

Unvoiced is performed here by bass violist, Liam Byrne.
