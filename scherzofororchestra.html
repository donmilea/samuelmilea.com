Scherzo for Orchestra

I composed this piece throughout the Summer of 2018, in preparation for a performance/workshop at the Royal Academy of Music.

At the time of writing I had recently reemerged from an intense infatuation with Stravinsky's ballet, the Firebird. As a consequence, I used my opportunity to write for a full orchestra as chance experiment with some of Stravinsky's orchestration techniques, but within the context of my own melodic and harmonic ideas.

Along with the Firebird, being a big inspiration, so too was Oliver Knussen's Flourish with Fireworks. I was preoccupied with Knussen's use of set-variation; a pitch-generating technique originally made popular by Stravinsky. I used this technique extensively in this piece, as a device to generate the melodic material.
